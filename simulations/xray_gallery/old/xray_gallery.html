X-ray Burst Image Gallery

This page contains recent results of X-ray burst simulations performed with the FLASH code. Images are presented in GIF and Postscript formats, and movies are available in QuickTime. This page will be updated as new results become available.

 

Helium Detonations on Neutron Stars: 2-dimensional Results
M. Zingale, F. X. Timmes, B. Fryxell, D. Q. Lamb, K. Olson, A. C. Calder, L. J. Dursi, P. Ricker, R. Rosner, P. MacNeice, and H. Tufo

Results from a 2-dimensional, cylindrical coordinates, X-ray burst calculation with the FLASH-1 code. The domain is a 2 km region along the surface of the neutron star (horizontal axis), extending 1.5 km radially outward (vertical axis). The initial model was a hydrostatic neutron star atmosphere, with a density of 2.e9 g/cc at the base, falling off to ~1.e6 g/cc over 100 meters. Above this, the density falls off quickly to 1.e-5 g/cc. The atmosphere and material above it are pure helium. We started the burst by raising the temperature in the lower left corner of the domain to 2.5e8 K, at which temperature thermonuclear burning of helium commenced, creating a detonation. In the vertical direction, the detonation wave propagated down the density gradient, breaking through the surface of the neutron star atmosphere. Above the atmosphere, the shock detached from the burning front and raced ahead. The detonation front moved along the surface of the star at about 1.e9 cm/s.

In all the pictures, a green line marks a helium abundance of 0.9; behind this line, burning has begun to deplete the helium. The dark blue line marks 10 g/cc, giving an indication of how much the neutron star atmosphere has been distorted by the explosion. Results for the temperature and density, at several times during the calculation, are presented, together with the AMR mesh used.

This calculation was performed on the Nirvana cluster (SGI Origin 2000) at Los Alamos National Laboratory.
