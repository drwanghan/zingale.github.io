Type I X-ray bursts

Led by former graduate student Chris Malone (now at UCSC) along with Ann Almgren, John Bell, and Andy Nonaka (CCSE/LBL) on modeling Type I X-ray bursts with Maestro
X-ray bursts are the thermonuclear explosion of a thin H/He layer on the surface of a neutron star. Our multi-dimensional simulations capture the dynamics of the convection leading up to the burst, using the low Mach number hydrodynamics code, MAESTRO. The calculations allow us to understand the role of the convection on the nucleosynthesis.


(supported by DOE Office of Nuclear Physics)

 
 		  
 

Maestro: A Low Mach Number Stellar Hydrodynamics Code

in collaboration with Ann Almgren, John Bell, and Andy Nonaka (CCSE/LBL)
Traditional astrophysical hydrodynamics codes have timesteps that are limited by the sound-crossing time across a zone. For very subsonic flows, this is inefficient. We developed a new algorithm for the efficient simulation of reaction-driven convection in stars. By filtering out soundwaves, we are able to evolve the star for long periods of time. The resulting code, Maestro, is publicly available.


(supported by DOE Office of Nuclear Physics)
